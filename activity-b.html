🍪 The Cookie Monster

Websites use small data files called cookies to remember who you are. Some websites store your user role — like guest or admin — directly in a cookie. If they don't protect it properly, anyone can just… change it.

🧠 What Just Happened?

The website stored your user role directly in a browser cookie with no cryptographic verification. When you refreshed, the server blindly read role=admin and gave you full access.

Real-world systems solve this with signed tokens (JWT) or server-side sessions — meaning the server keeps track of who you are, not your browser. If anyone tampers with the cookie, the signature breaks and access is denied.

This vulnerability is called Insecure Direct Object Reference (IDOR) and it has caused major real-world breaches.
